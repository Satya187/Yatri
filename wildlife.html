Nagarahole National Park, Karnataka

Nagarahole National Park, also known as Rajiv Gandhi National Park, is a beautiful wildlife reserve known for its rich biodiversity, including tigers, elephants, and diverse bird species.

Kaziranga National Park, Assam

Kaziranga National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the last remaining habitats of the Indian one-horned rhinoceros. It is also home to tigers, elephants, and various bird species.

Keoladeo National Park, Rajasthan

Formerly known as Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary, Keoladeo National Park is a paradise for birdwatchers, with over 370 bird species recorded. It is also home to various mammals, reptiles, and amphibians.

Satpura National Park, Madhya Pradesh

Satpura National Park is known for its diverse wildlife and scenic beauty, offering opportunities for wildlife safaris, trekking, and boating. It is home to tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and many other species.

Hemis National Park, Jammu and Kashmir

Hemis National Park is the largest national park in India and a high-altitude park that provides habitat to several endangered species, including the snow leopard. It is also home to various bird species and rare flora.
